The Megaminx

The Megaminx is a Rubik's Cube in the shape of a dodecahedron - it has 12 pentagonal faces that turn.

It was invented in 1982 by Uwe Meffert and produced by the company Meffert's.

We like the megaminx because it is fun to solve. Many people believe it is the best twisty puzzle.

It was added to the World Cube Association in 2003.

There are 12-coloured versions which are normal, and 6-colour versions which aren't legal in competitions, so they don't make them anymore.

The world record is 23.18 seconds, set by Leandro Martίn López of Argentina in April 2024.

How do you solve the Megaminx?

First you make a white star, with the edgepiece colours lining up with the side centers, then you solve the corners and edges of the first two layers(F2L).

Then you make the star on one of the side colours (often yellow) and solve the second two layers (S2L) around it.

Repeat for the rest of the side colours. The last two are a little different, but when you've completed them, only the last layer remains. you solve it similarily to 3x3 beginner method.
