The 3x3

The 3x3 is the normal Rubik's Cube invented by Ernő Rubik on May 17 1974.

It was originally going to be a object for his students to show how 3d movement worked, but instead was chosen to be produced as a toy.

It took Ernő a month to figure out how to solve it before it was made as a toy. Most people can't solve it at all, so a month to figure it out is incredible speed. The current world record for solving the 3x3 is 3.13 seconds set by Max Park on 11 June 2023.
